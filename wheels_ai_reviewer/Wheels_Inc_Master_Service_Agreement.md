## MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Master Service Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date set out on the cover
page, by and between Wheels Inc., a corporation duly organized and existing under applicable law, having its
principal place of business at [Wheels Inc. Address] ("Wheels", the "Lessor", or the "Service Provider"), and
[Client Legal Name], a company duly organized and existing under applicable law, having its principal place of
business at [Client Address] (the "Client" or the "Lessee"). Wheels and the Client are each referred to
individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties".


### Recitals

WHEREAS, Wheels is in the business of providing fleet management services, including vehicle leasing and the
administration of fuel, mileage, toll, telematics, insurance, maintenance, violation management, remarketing,
and related programs for commercial vehicle fleets;

WHEREAS, the Client desires to engage Wheels to provide certain of such fleet management services in respect
of Vehicles leased by the Client from Wheels under the companion Master Lease Agreement, and Wheels is
willing to provide such services, in each case subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement;

WHEREAS, this Agreement governs the terms on which the Services described herein are ordered, delivered,
calculated, invoiced, and paid, and is intended to be read together with the Master Lease Agreement and all
Schedules, Vehicle Schedules, and Service Election Forms executed by the Parties from time to time;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, and for other good and valuable
consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:


## ARTICLE 1 - DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION


### 1.1 Definitions

For purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below. Capitalized terms
used but not defined in this Agreement shall have the meanings given to them in the Master Lease Agreement.

attached hereto, as the same may be amended, restated, or supplemented from time to time.

"Billing Cycle" means the recurring monthly period, ending on the last calendar day of each month unless
otherwise specified in a Vehicle Schedule, in respect of which Wheels calculates and issues an Invoice for
Services rendered.

"Effective Date" means the date first written on the cover page of this Agreement, being the date on which
this Agreement becomes binding on the Parties.

"Fleet" means collectively, all Vehicles leased by the Client from Wheels under the Master Lease Agreement
and enrolled for one or more Services under this Agreement.

"Invoice" means a monthly statement issued by Wheels to the Client itemizing all Rentals and Service charges
due for the relevant Billing Cycle, prepared in accordance with Article 5.

as transferring title to, or any ownership interest in, any Vehicle to the Client.

## ARTICLE 3 - TERM AND RENEWAL


### 3.1 Initial Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for an initial term of thirty-six (36)
months (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with Article 12.


### 3.2 Renewal

Following the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive periods of twelve (12)
months each (each, a "Renewal Term", and together with the Initial Term, the "Term"), unless either Party
provides the other with written notice of non-renewal at least ninety (90) days prior to the expiration of the
then-current Term.

Termination or expiration of this Agreement shall not affect any rights or obligations of either Party which have
accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including any unpaid Invoices, and Articles 1, 5.6, 9, 10, 11,
12.4, and 14 shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE 5 - SERVICES AND CALCULATION METHODOLOGY

This Article 5 describes the five (5) Services offered by Wheels under this Agreement, including the standard
methodology used to calculate charges for each Service. The standard rates referenced below are set out in
full in Schedule A, and worked numeric examples are set out in Schedule B. Rates may be varied for a given
Client or Vehicle only by a duly executed Vehicle Schedule or written amendment referencing this Agreement.


### 5.1 Fuel Program

Wheels shall issue a fuel card or equivalent payment mechanism for each Vehicle enrolled in the Fuel Program,
enabling the driver to purchase fuel at participating fuel network locations. Wheels shall pay the fuel network
directly and shall pass through the actual cost of fuel purchased to the Client, together with a program
administration fee per transaction.

Standard Calculation: Monthly Fuel Charge = 2 (Gallons or Litres Purchased x Price per Unit at Point of Sale,
for each transaction in the Billing Cycle) + (Number of Fuel Transactions in the Billing Cycle x Program
Administration Fee).

Wheels reserves the right to flag transactions for manual review where fuel volume purchased in a single
transaction exceeds the reasonable tank capacity of the relevant Vehicle, or where purchase frequency is
inconsistent with reported mileage under the Mileage & Logging Program, as a fraud and misuse control.


### 5.2 Mileage & Logging Program

Each Vehicle is assigned an annual mileage allowance under its Vehicle Schedule (the standard allowance being
15,000 miles per annum unless otherwise specified). The Client shall ensure that odometer readings are
submitted monthly, either through self-reporting or automatically via the Telematics Program where enrolled,
to enable Wheels to maintain an accurate mileage log for each Vehicle for both billing and tax/compliance
reporting purposes.

Standard Calculation: Monthly Excess Mileage Charge = MAX[0, Actual Odometer Miles Driven in the Billing
Cycle - (Annual Mileage Allowance # 12)] x Excess Mileage Rate, plus a fixed monthly Mileage Log & Reporting
Fee per Vehicle.

Unused mileage allowance in a given month does not carry forward to subsequent months but is reconciled
across the full Lease Term at Lease-end, such that excess mileage charges are calculated by reference to the
cumulative allowance for the elapsed Lease Term against cumulative actual miles driven, less amounts already
invoiced during the Term.


### 5.3 Toll Program

Wheels shall arrange for a toll transponder or equivalent electronic toll collection device to be affixed to each
Vehicle enrolled in the Toll Program. Wheels shall settle toll charges directly with the relevant toll authorities
and shall pass through actual toll charges to the Client, together with a per-transaction handling fee and a fixed
monthly transponder account fee.

Standard Calculation: Monthly Toll Charge = { (Toll Authority Charges incurred in the Billing Cycle) + (Number
of Toll Transactions x Transaction Handling Fee) + Fixed Transponder Account Fee.

The Client shall be responsible for any toll violation, administrative penalty, or late fee levied by a toll authority
arising from failure of the transponder to register a lawful toll transaction, except to the extent such failure is
caused by a defect in the transponder attributable to Wheels.


### 5.4 Telematics Program

Wheels shall install, or arrange for the installation of, a telematics device on each Vehicle enrolled in the
Telematics Program, enabling location tracking, driver behavior monitoring, and (for the Advanced Diagnostics
Package) vehicle health and diagnostic data collection. Telematics data may also be used to support mileage
verification under the Mileage & Logging Program and safety monitoring under other Services elected by the
Client.

Standard Calculation: Monthly Telematics Charge = Fixed Package Rate (Standard or Advanced, as elected on
the Service Election Form) per Vehicle, pro-rated on a 1/30th-per-day basis for any Vehicle enrolled or de-
enrolled partway through a Billing Cycle.

Data collected through the Telematics Program shall be handled in accordance with Article 9 (Confidentiality
and Data Protection) and shall be used by Wheels solely for fleet management, billing verification, and safety
purposes, and for such other purposes as the Client separately authorizes in writing.


### 5.5 Insurance Program

Where the Client elects the Insurance Program for a Vehicle, Wheels shall arrange and maintain liability and
physical damage insurance coverage for such Vehicle at all times during the Lease Term, in amounts and on
terms consistent with Wheels' standard fleet insurance program and applicable law. The Client may
additionally elect a Physical Damage Waiver, under which Wheels waives its right to recover from the Client
for physical damage to the Vehicle in excess of the applicable deductible, subject to the exclusions set out in
the Master Lease Agreement (including gross negligence, willful misconduct, and unauthorized use).

Standard Calculation: Monthly Insurance Charge = Rate-Class Premium (determined by the Vehicle's Rate
Class, A through D, as set out in Schedule C to the Master Lease Agreement) + Physical Damage Waiver Fee, if
elected.

## ARTICLE 6 - BILLING, INVOICING, AND PAYMENT


### 6.1 Monthly Invoicing

Wheels shall issue a single consolidated Invoice to the Client for each Billing Cycle, itemizing (a) Rentals due
under the Master Lease Agreement, and (b) charges for each Service elected by the Client, calculated in
accordance with Article 5 and Schedule A, broken down by Vehicle.


### 6.2 Payment Terms

The Client shall pay each Invoice in full within thirty (30) days of the Invoice date ("Due Date"). Amounts not
paid by the Due Date shall accrue interest at the lesser of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or the
maximum rate permitted by applicable law, calculated from the Due Date until paid in full.


### 6.3 Disputed Charges

If the Client disputes any portion of an Invoice in good faith, the Client shall notify Wheels in writing within
fifteen (15) days of the Invoice date, specifying the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute. The Client
shall pay all undisputed amounts by the Due Date. The Parties shall use reasonable efforts to resolve any billing
dispute within thirty (30) days of notice, and any amount finally determined to be due shall be paid within ten
(10) days of such determination, together with interest accrued under Article 6.2 from the original Due Date.


### 6.4 Reconciliation and Adjustment

Charges for the Fuel Program, Toll Program, and Mileage & Logging Program are based on actual usage data
received by Wheels from fuel networks, toll authorities, and odometer or telematics reports, respectively, and
may include adjustments in a subsequent Billing Cycle to correct for late-arriving usage data, provided that
Wheels shall not adjust charges more than ninety (90) days after the original usage occurred, except in the case
of fraud or manifest error.

If any undisputed amount remains unpaid more than thirty (30) days after the Due Date, Wheels may, upon

## ARTICLE 7 - FEES, RATE ADJUSTMENTS, AND PASS-THROUGH COSTS


### 7.1 Standard Rates

The standard fees and rates for each Service as of the Effective Date are set out in Schedule A. Pass-through
costs (fuel, tolls, and insurance claims-related costs) reflect Wheels' actual third-party cost and are not marked
up except as expressly set out in Schedule A.


### 7.2 Annual Adjustment

Wheels may adjust the fixed-fee components of the Services set out in Schedule A (excluding pass-through
costs) once per twelve (12)-month period by no more than the greater of three percent (3%) or the percentage
increase in the U.S. Consumer Price Index (All Urban Consumers) over the preceding twelve (12) months, upon
no less than sixty (60) days' prior written notice to the Client.


### 7.3 Extraordinary Adjustments

Where a material and sustained change in the cost of providing a Service occurs outside the ordinary course
(including significant fuel market disruption or insurance market hardening), Wheels may propose an
extraordinary rate adjustment upon ninety (90) days' written notice, which adjustment shall take effect unless
the Client terminates the affected Service in accordance with Article 12.3 prior to the effective date of such
adjustment.

## ARTICLE 9 - WHEELS' OBLIGATIONS AND SERVICE LEVELS


### 9.1 Standard of Performance

representative Vehicle over a single Billing Cycle. These examples are illustrative only and do not modify the
definitive rates set out in Schedule A or in any applicable Vehicle Schedule.

</tr>
<tr>
<td>Mileage &amp; Logging</td>
<td>Excess Mileage Charge = MAX[0, (Actual Odometer Miles - (Annual Allowance + 12))] x USD 0.12, plus fixed Logging Fee of USD 3.50</td>
<td>Actual miles this month = 1,450. Monthly allowance = 15,000 + 12 = 1,250. Excess = 200 miles x USD 0.12 = USD 24.00, plus USD 3.50 logging fee. Total = USD 27.50</td>

<tr>
